Class of 2014 • October 12, 2013

The Night of the Library Lock-in

Sarah Jenkins Political Science

It started as a study group for the finals, but turned into an adventure when we realized we were locked in. The silence of the stacks at 2 AM was different. It wasn't just quiet; it was heavy with the scent of old paper and the hum of the vending machine downstairs.

We gathered our snacks—stale pretzels and lukewarm coffee—and decided that if we were stuck, we might as well make the most of it. Someone found an old rolling ladder, and for the next three hours, we weren't stressed students anymore. We were explorers in a labyrinth of knowledge.

Class of 2012 • Spring Break 2011

Road Trip to the Coast

Marcus Chen Engineering

We packed five people into a four-person sedan. The AC was broken, and the only CD we had was a scratched mix from 2005. But as we hit the highway, windows down, none of that mattered.

I remember stopping at that weird diner with the neon sign flickering in the twilight. It felt like a scene from a movie. We talked about our futures as if we had it all figured out, blissfully unaware of how much—and how little—would change.

Class of 2015 • May 20, 2015

Graduation Day Chaos

Emily Rossi Fine Arts

It rained. Not just a drizzle, but a torrential downpour right as the ceremony started. We were soaked in our gowns, mud splattering our nice shoes. Everyone was running for cover, laughing hysterically.
